TCD by jeffWelling

Traffic Control Daemon

Whats TCD?

TCD stands for Traffic Control Daemon.  TCD collects statistics from your internet gateway(s) and will help you avoid bandwidth overage charges from your ISP using user-defined rules.  Rules are composed of conditionals, and commands that are executed when those conditions are met.  This can help people with a single internet connection as well as multi-homed users.  Example uses include...

You have one internet connection and are allowed 60 Gigabytes of traffic per month.  You download a lot of content, but aren't interested in upgrading your internet connection to accomodate the only occasional overage.
To help make sure you don't go over your 60 Gigabyte allotment, you can use TCD.
For this, you could configure TCD such that at 98% usage (a little wiggle room is always nice), a command is run to throttle your internet connection down to a trickle.  By also inputting the date of your billing cycle rollover, TCD can then run another command when your billing cycle rolls over to un-throttle your connection to give you full speeds again.

And...
You have two internet connections.  Connection A allows you 80 Gigabytes per month and connection B allows you 120 Gigabytes.  Your regular monthly traffic easily reaches 100 Gigabytes, more than connection A allows, but because connection B is less reliable or has higher latency you prefer to use connection A whenever possible.
You could use TCD to monitor both connections, giving it the max capacity, rollover date, and some rules. 
Rules so that when connection A reaches 98% capacity, connection B becomes the new default gateway, and so that when connection A rolls over it becomes the default gateway again.  And if going over your usage on connection B is also a concern you can add a rule so that when connection B's usage reaches 98% a command is run to throttle the connection down to a trickle, and another to undo the throttling when connection B rolls over.
